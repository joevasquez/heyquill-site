Dictate anywhere. Locally. Instantly.

Quill is a privacy-first voice-to-text suite for Apple devices. On your Mac, hold a hotkey to dictate into any app. On your iPhone, hit record to build running voice notes. Either way, transcription happens locally — by state-of-the-art speech models on-device. No audio ever leaves your Mac or iPhone.

Optional AI clean-up can polish your raw dictation into clean prose, structured notes, a professional email, or code comments — using your own OpenAI or Anthropic API key. Available in both the Mac and iOS apps.

About

Quill is my take on what a dictation app should be: fast, local, keyboard-driven, and respectful of what you're saying. What started on the Mac now has a companion iPhone app for capturing voice notes on the go.

On the Mac, Quill lives in your menu bar. You press your hotkey, speak, release, and the transcript gets pasted directly into the app you're already using — Messages, Mail, a code editor, a terminal, wherever. On iPhone, Quill is a voice-notes app: hit record, speak, and each clip stitches into a running note. On both platforms, transcription runs entirely on-device using Apple's Core ML with Parakeet (multilingual, macOS) or Whisper (macOS + iOS). Your audio never touches a cloud server.

If you want more than a raw transcript, Quill — on either platform — can route the text through OpenAI or Anthropic in one of six modes: Clean, Email, Notes, Message, Code, or Off. On the Mac you can also tell Quill to pick the right mode automatically based on which app is in front. You bring your own API key; the app just plumbs the request.

macOS

What Quill for Mac does

On-device transcription

  • Parakeet TDT v3 (multilingual, default) via FluidAudio
  • Whisper Tiny / Base / Large v3 via WhisperKit
  • Core ML accelerated — no network round-trip, no server cost

Hotkey-driven workflow

  • Press-and-hold or double-tap-to-lock modes
  • Modifier-only hotkeys (Fn, Option, etc.) with safety thresholds
  • Automatic paste into the frontmost app — no window switch

Optional AI post-processing

  • Six modes: Clean, Email, Notes, Message, Code, or Off
  • Context-aware mode selection per frontmost app
  • Bring your own OpenAI / Anthropic key — stored in Keychain
  • Inline voice commands ("new paragraph", "period", "question mark")
iOS · TestFlight beta

What Quill for iPhone does

Quill iOS is the mobile companion to the Mac app. It's not a port — it's a voice-note app built for the moments you can't reach a keyboard. Hit record, speak, release, and the transcript appends to whatever note you're building. Record a conference talk in sections and see the whole note grow. Same six AI modes as the desktop app (Clean, Email, Notes, Message, Code, Off). Same on-device transcription — your voice never touches a cloud server.

On-device transcription

  • Runs Whisper locally on your iPhone via Core ML
  • Live partial transcript via Apple's SFSpeechRecognizer, replaced by final Whisper output on stop
  • No audio ever leaves the device for transcription

Notes that stitch together

  • Each recording appends to your active note with a paragraph break
  • Rough starting location tagged (neighborhood, not exact address)
  • Full list of all notes — swap, delete, swipe back in time

Built for speed

  • Custom record button with a level-reactive glow
  • Haptics on start / stop / copy / mode change
  • Green checkmark animation on copy
  • iOS share sheet — Messages, Mail, Notes, Slack, anywhere

Privacy

Your audio never leaves your device.

On both Mac and iPhone, transcription runs locally on-device using Core ML. If you enable AI clean-up, only the transcribed text is sent to OpenAI or Anthropic — using your own API key, stored in Keychain — and only after you've opted in.

Requirements

Quill for Mac

  • macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later, Apple Silicon or Intel
  • ~75 MB to ~1.5 GB of disk for your chosen speech model (downloaded on first use)
  • Microphone, Accessibility, and Input Monitoring permissions (standard for any dictation app)

Quill for iPhone

  • iPhone or iPad running iOS 26.2 or later
  • TestFlight app installed (currently in public beta)
  • Microphone, Speech Recognition, and Location (optional) permissions
